Overview

The Problem

A challenger bank was launching in a crowded market. Their MVP had solid back-end infrastructure but an experience that felt generic, untrustworthy, and cognitively exhausting. Users were dropping off during onboarding at 68%. Those who completed it were churning within 30 days at a rate of 54%.

Trust and clarity were the two missing ingredients. My job was to design both into the product — through every interaction, micro-animation, and piece of copy.

Research

Understanding Trust in Financial Apps

I ran 20 user interviews focused specifically on trust signals in financial apps — what made people feel safe, what made them feel anxious. Trust in fintech is built through: speed of feedback, transparency of state, visual predictability, and absence of surprise. Every design decision was filtered through this lens.

"If the app hesitates for even half a second when I move money, I panic. That's my rent."

Design

Designing for Trust at Every Touchpoint

The redesign touched every layer: onboarding was rebuilt as a progressive confidence-building sequence (not a form dump). Transaction confirmations got Lottie animations that communicated finality with warmth. Balance views were simplified to eliminate the cognitive effort of interpretation. Every error state was rewritten to be human, not technical.
