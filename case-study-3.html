Overview

The Problem

A growing SaaS operations team was spending 60% of their day managing a document approval workflow that touched 6 different systems and required 14 discrete steps — many of which existed purely as legacy process artifacts with no functional purpose.

My approach: map the entire workflow against actual business logic, identify which steps created value and which created only friction, then redesign the flow around outcomes — not inherited process.

Process

Mapping, Cutting, Redesigning

I ran a 2-day process mapping workshop with the team, documenting every step and its actual purpose. Of 14 steps, 7 were redundant or legacy. We collapsed the 7 remaining into 4 consolidated actions — each with clear ownership, automatic status updates, and exception handling built in.

The redesigned workflow was implemented in Airtable with a Figma-designed UI layer — keeping the backend familiar while making the user experience dramatically simpler.
